Source title: Ohio State Incoming Students Housing Page
Source type: official OSU housing page
URL: https://housing.osu.edu/incoming-students/

It says that living on campus offers advantages and is intended to support student success at Ohio State. Campus housing is home to first-year and upperclass students.

The page says all unmarried, full-time students within two years of high school graduation must live on campus unless they are exempted, space is no longer available, or they are living with family. This is important for questions about whether first-year or second-year students are required to live in university housing.

Ohio State lists several advantages of on-campus housing. These include convenient location to classes, resident advisors assigned to every floor to help students transition to college and live with roommates, full-time professional staff living in each housing complex, opportunities to develop friendships and memories, and opportunities to get involved in student life. The page also points students toward Learning Communities if they want a special living arrangement based on academic, cultural, or lifestyle interests.

The page also discusses disability accommodations. Students requesting housing modifications as disability accommodations should contact Student Life Disability Services. Students who need special assignment consideration for other reasons, or students with a service animal or therapy animal, are encouraged to contact Housing and Residence Education to discuss options.
